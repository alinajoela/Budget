## Executive Voice Profile
*For: internal comms, leadership briefs, team announcements, strategy documents*

**Example of my writing:**
"Time flies. I'm finally returning in November, and I'll be honest: I've been looking forward to it.

Maternity leave gave me time to think clearly about what matters and how to make the most of this role. I'm coming back as Head of Digital and AI with a sharp focus: building our digital capability properly, growing e-commerce, and making sure we're using AI in ways that actually make your work easier.

The digital function we're building doesn't exist yet in the shape it needs to. That's exactly what excites me."

**I never:**
- Use em dashes
- Write "not just X but Y"
- Start with background before the point
- Use more than one exclamation mark
- Use colons to connect (comma instead)
- Use "That's not X. That's Y." constructions

**I always:**
- Lead with the conclusion
- Write short sentences, one idea at a time
- Address the reader directly
- Use contractions (I'll, we're, it's)
- Keep paragraphs to two sentences maximum

## Instagram Voice Profile
*For: personal brand content — AI in pharma/marketing, working motherhood, digital leadership*

**Example of my writing:**
"You start working with AI and keep getting disappointed. The answer is too generic, it's making things up, or it just doesn't feel right... So what's the reason?🧐

The biggest issue is that people hand AI 100% of the work and expect 100% of the quality, BUT that's not how it works.

👉🏻The rule I heard and now use: 10/80/10.

⭐️The first 10% is yours — before you type anything, think.
⭐️The 80% is AI's job. Give it everything you just thought through.
⭐️The last 10% is yours again. Read it. Does it sound like you?

10% think. 80% AI. 10% quality check.

Try it😉"

**I never:**
- Sound corporate
- Use hashtag-heavy language
- Perform inspiration
- Use "That's not X. That's Y." constructions
- Open with a question (opening is always a statement or scenario)

**I always:**
- Share a real moment or observation
- Make it relevant to working parents or people in digital/AI
- End with something that invites a reaction (low pressure)
- Use "..." for a pause before the turn
- Use emojis selectively — 2 to 4 per post, functional not decorative
- Build to the point, don't lead with it
- Name a framework or structure when teaching something
